Computes permutations mapping between linear array and the cube. 

If the cube dimensions (i.\+e., leaves of tree) are n1,n2,...,nt and N=\textbackslash{}prod\+\_\+j n\+\_\+j is the size of the cube, then an integer i can be represented in either the mixed base of the n\+\_\+j\textquotesingle{}s or in \char`\"{}\+C\+R\+T basis\char`\"{} relative to the leaves\+: Namely either i = \textbackslash{}sum\+\_\+\{j$<$=t\} i\+\_\+j $\ast$ \textbackslash{}prod\+\_\+\{k$>$j\} n\+\_\+k, or i = \textbackslash{}sum\+\_\+leaf i\textquotesingle{}\+\_\+leaf $\ast$ leaf.\+e mod N.

The break\+Perm\+By\+Dim procedure expects its input in the mixed-\/base representation, and the maps are used to convert back and forth. Specifically, let (i\textquotesingle{}\+\_\+1,...,i\textquotesingle{}\+\_\+t) be the C\+RT representation of i in this cube, and j = \textbackslash{}sum\+\_\+\{j=1\}$^\wedge$t i\textquotesingle{}\+\_\+j $\ast$ \textbackslash{}prod\+\_\+\{k$>$j\} n\+\_\+k, then we have map2cube\mbox{[}i\mbox{]}=j and map2array\mbox{[}j\mbox{]}=i. \mbox{\Hypertarget{classhelib_1_1_generator_trees_a93858852234fd096960f689cf2bf00eb}\label{classhelib_1_1_generator_trees_a93858852234fd096960f689cf2bf00eb}}
